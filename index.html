Trap energy distribution in aircraft dielectric materials through surface potential decay method

Dong Chen, XiaoFeng Hu *, Lei Wang, Ming Wei, JianPing Zhang, Shuai Zhou
Army Engineering University of PLA, Shijiazhuang Campus, Shijiazhuang, 050003, China
Abstract

Charge deposition on aircraft insulation material surfaces is challenging to discharge via electrostatic discharger, potentially leading to streamer discharge and interference with avionic systems. To mitigate this risk and enhance flight safety, this study investigates the trap energy distribution in aircraft dielectric materials using the surface potential decay method. Our research provides critical insights into charge trapping mechanisms and their implications for aircraft electrical system reliability.

Keywords: Aircraft dielectric materials, Surface potential decay, Trap energy distribution, Charge trapping, Electrical insulation
